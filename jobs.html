My Relationship with the Job Market

Hello there. First and foremost, thank you for scrolling down this far and taking interest in my profile. The truth is, I've been looking for a software development job for a while now. Day after day, rejection after rejection, I keep testing my luck and hoping to get a junior software development position.

In 2022, I was interviewed for a job at a certain company for Python development - good salary, interesting responsabilities, and so on. I was actually interested, but they wanted me to drop my degree and work with them fulltime, which is something I simply couldn't do at the time. I was still trying to get my minor in chemistry, but ended up dropping it due to time constraints and I ended up replacing it with several computer science classes and projects in order to obtain a partial computer science minor.

In late 2022 and early 2023, I had the opportunity to work with a fantastic group at McGill working on the ALBATROS (Array of Long Baseline Antennas for Taking Radio Observations from the Sub-antarctic/Seventy-ninth parallel) under professor Cynthia Chiang.
